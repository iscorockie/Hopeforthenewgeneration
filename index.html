About Hope for the Next Generation

Building a brighter future for children across Mbale and beyond

Our Story

Hope for the Next Generation (HNG) was founded in 2020 by Uganda Rugby Cranes captain Ivan Magomu, a passionate advocate for child empowerment and community development. Born from a deep desire to give back to his hometown of Mbale, Ivan established HNG to address the critical gaps in education, sports access, and child protection facing vulnerable children in Eastern Uganda.

What began as a small community sports initiative has grown into a comprehensive child-focused organisation reaching over 1,250 children across 12 communities. Our programmes combine the power of grassroots rugby and sports with quality education support, child protection advocacy, and girls' empowerment — creating holistic pathways for children to thrive.

"Every child deserves a chance to dream, to play, to learn, and to grow. Sport taught me discipline and resilience — I want every child in Mbale to have that same opportunity."
— Ivan Magomu, Founder & CEO

Our Vision

A world where every child in Mbale and beyond has access to quality education, safe spaces for play and development, and the opportunity to reach their full potential regardless of their background.

Our Mission

To restore hope and unlock potential among vulnerable children and youth through grassroots sports, quality education, talent development, and sustainable livelihoods — creating confident, resilient leaders for tomorrow.

Comprehensive programmes transforming lives across Mbale and beyond

01 / Education & Life Skills

Education & Life Skills

Quality education and school-support initiatives that nurture young minds and break cycles of intergenerational poverty in Mbale City and surrounding communities.

In Eastern Uganda, school dropout rates remain alarmingly high — particularly for girls, children with disabilities, and those from the most marginalised households. Many families cannot afford school materials, and over-stretched school systems leave at-risk learners behind.

HNG delivers targeted tutoring, mentorship, life-skills workshops, and school materials provision. Our after-school programme creates a safe, structured space where children thrive and build confidence for lifelong learning.

450+ Children in after-school
87% Retention rate
12 Partner schools
200+ Kits distributed
02 / Grassroots Sports

Grassroots Sports & Talent Development

Rugby and community sports are used as powerful tools for discipline, teamwork, physical wellness, and holistic child development. HNG uses the playing field as a classroom.

Sport is one of the most powerful vehicles for change — yet for many children in Mbale, access to quality coaching, equipment, and safe playing spaces remains out of reach. Without mentors, raw talent goes unrecognised and undeveloped.

HNG partners with Uganda Rugby Union and community coaches to deliver structured training, inter-school leagues, and talent exposure events. We coach both athletic excellence and life values: respect, perseverance, and cooperation.

600+ Youth in sports
8 Schools covered
45% Female participants
3 National players
03 / Child Protection

Child Protection & Juvenile Justice

Advocacy for child rights, prevention of abuse, and rehabilitation support for affected children and caregivers in Mbale communities.

Many children in Mbale face significant protection risks including child labour, early marriage, physical abuse, and limited access to justice. Vulnerable children often fall through the cracks of formal protection systems.

HNG works with local authorities, schools, and communities to strengthen child protection mechanisms. We provide psychosocial support, legal aid referrals, and rehabilitation programmes for children in contact with the law.

04 / Girls Empowerment

Girls Empowerment & Inclusion

Girls' camps, skills-building workshops, and livelihood training that promote gender equality and social inclusion across Mbale communities.

Girls in Eastern Uganda face disproportionate barriers to education, sports participation, and economic opportunity. Cultural norms, early pregnancy, and household responsibilities often limit their potential.

HNG's girls' empowerment programme creates safe spaces for girls to learn, play, and lead. Through sports camps, menstrual health education, and vocational skills training, we help girls build confidence and challenge limiting norms.

05 / Community

Community Sustainability & Livelihoods

Training and collaborative projects addressing poverty, health, environment, and livelihood improvement for families across Mbale.

Sustainable change requires supporting not just children, but their families and communities. Economic instability, poor health outcomes, and environmental challenges undermine children's wellbeing.

HNG implements community-based projects including parent support groups, agricultural training, and health awareness campaigns. By strengthening families, we create the stable foundation children need to thrive.
